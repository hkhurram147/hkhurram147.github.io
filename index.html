Research

Scalable Clustering Algorithm

MILP Optimization Python Visualization

Challenge

Traditional clustering algorithms struggled with large operational datasets and outlier sensitivity.

Approach

Developed a novel clustering algorithm using mixed-integer programming for large operational datasets (1MM+ data points).

Outcome

Co-authored publication accepted for AAAI 2023 Conference, created a more robust clustering method that handles outliers and cluster imbalances better.

This research project addressed fundamental limitations in existing clustering algorithms. Working in the Data-Driven Decision Making Lab at the University of Toronto, I developed a novel approach to the k-center clustering problem using mixed-integer linear programming.

Key innovations included:

  • Formulation of the generalized L1 k-center clustering problem as a MILP
  • Implementation of an efficient constraint generation algorithm
  • Theoretical analysis of solution optimality and complexity
  • Empirical evaluation across synthetic and real-world datasets
  • Benchmarking against SOTA clustering algorithms

The research demonstrated that our approach achieved global optimality with significantly better robustness to outliers compared to heuristic methods. The paper was accepted for publication at the AAAI 2023 Conference, one of the premier venues in artificial intelligence research.

UofT MI Club

Audio Sequence Modeling for Noise Pollution

Deep Learning Audio Processing Spectrograms TensorFlow

Challenge

Environmental agencies needed better tools to detect and classify noise pollution in urban environments.

Approach

Optimized a pre-trained deep learning model for noise pollution detection and managed data ingestion for processing audio spectrograms.

Outcome

Boosted classification accuracy by 20%, delivering data-driven solutions to support environmental decision-making for Aercoustics Engineering Ltd.

As project lead for the University of Toronto Machine Intelligence Club, I led a team of 5 students to develop an audio classification system for environmental noise monitoring. This project was conducted in collaboration with Aercoustics Engineering Ltd.

Our technical approach included:

  • Collection and preprocessing of 1000+ hours of urban audio data
  • Feature extraction using mel-spectrograms and MFCCs
  • Fine-tuning of pre-trained CNN and transformer architectures
  • Implementation of data augmentation techniques for robustness
  • Deployment of models on edge devices for real-time monitoring

The final system could classify 12 different urban noise types with 92% accuracy, representing a 20% improvement over baseline methods. The project resulted in a deployed solution that helps environmental agencies monitor and regulate noise pollution in urban settings.
